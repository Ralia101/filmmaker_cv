About The Filmmaker

Rahman Oladigbolu is a writer, director, and producer with a passion for telling stories that inspire and uplift. Born in Nigeria, he moved to the United States to pursue his dream of becoming a filmmaker. After overcoming a seven-year illness, he began his career in Boston as a production assistant on films and television shows.

Rahman's journey in the film industry has been marked by determination and perseverance. He has worked on various projects, including experimental short films, and has written and directed two feature films, Soul Sisters and Theory of Conflict. His work explores themes of identity, culture, and the human experience, often drawing from his own experiences as an immigrant and a person of faith.

Rahman's films have received recognition and awards, including the Best Emerging Filmmaker Award at the Roxbury International Film Festival and the Artist Award at the American Islamic Congress. His work has been screened at film festivals and cultural institutes around the world, including the Cannes Pan-African Film Festival and the Werkstatt Der Kulturen institute in Germany.

Through his films, Rahman aims to bridge cultural divides and inspire audiences to embrace their unique stories and experiences. He is committed to creating content that promotes understanding, empathy, and unity.
